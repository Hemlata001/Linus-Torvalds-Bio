Linus Torvalds

The Creator of Linux

Biography

Linus Benedict Torvalds is a Finnish American software engineer who is the creator and lead developer of the Linux kernel, used by Linux distributions like Debian, Arch and Android. He also created the distributed version control system Git.Torvalds was born on December 28,1969 in Helsinki,Finland.He studied computer science at the University of Helsinki,and he developed the Linux Kernel as a student project in 1991.Since then,the Linux Kernel has become one of the most widely used kernels in the world,powering everything from smartphones to supercomputers.

  • 2010-Received the 2010 Millennium Technology Prize and the 2012 Free Software Foundation's Award for the Advancement of Free Software
  • 2013-Inducted into the Internet HAll of Frame in 2013 for his contributions to the development of the Linux Kernel and the cretion of Git

  • 2021-Received the 2021 IEEE masura Ibuka Consumer Electronics Award,which recognizes individulas who have make significance contributions tothe field of consumer electronics.
